\title{Nested Hubbard--Stratonovich Representations for Non-Gaussian Influence Functionals}
\author{Gerard McCaul}
\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
We present a commuting-sector focused formulation of nested Hubbard--Stratonovich (HS) representations for non-Gaussian influence functionals. For a system-bath Hamiltonian $H=H_S+H_B+f\otimes B$, the imaginary-time cumulant expansion of the reduced equilibrium operator is reorganised as a hierarchy of Gaussian HS channels acting on composite operators. In the commuting sector $[H_S,f]=0$, this hierarchy re-averages into a polynomial Hamiltonian of mean force,
\(
H_{\mathrm{MF}}=H_S-\sum_{n\ge 1}\alpha_{2n}f^{2n},
\)
with coefficients fixed by integrated bath cumulants obtained from derivatives of a source-deformed bath partition function. We validate the construction in two commuting benchmarks: (i) a Gaussian qutrit model with finite-mode bosonic exact diagonalisation and stochastic HS overlays, and (ii) a non-Gaussian qutrit model coupled linearly to a finite transverse-spin bath, where exact diagonalisation is compared to cumulant truncation ladders $K_2$, $K_2{+}K_4$, and $K_2{+}K_4{+}K_6$. Across the sampled parameter range, higher-order cumulants appear as controlled renormalisation corrections and systematically reduce residual error.
\end{abstract}
